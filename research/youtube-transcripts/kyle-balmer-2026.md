## Why I chose this source
Kyle Balmer covers the practical economics of AI for creators and 
knowledge workers — not hype, but real workflow and cost implications. 
This video is directly relevant to B2B SaaS teams making decisions about 
AI tool budgets and infrastructure. His take on Jevons Paradox and the 
death of all-you-can-eat AI pricing is one of the most practical 2026 
perspectives available.


## Key Insights
- Jevons Paradox — cheaper AI per token = higher total usage = higher bills
- Inference is the real bottleneck, not model intelligence
- All-you-can-eat subscription model is dying — metered pricing incoming
- Codex as alternative to Claude Code for more generous limits
- Token hygiene — use smaller models for small tasks, cache, trim context
- Local models via LM Studio as subscription-independent alternative
