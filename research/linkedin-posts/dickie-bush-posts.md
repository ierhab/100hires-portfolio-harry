Use this 4-step framework to generate $100k digital product ideas in under an hour.

To do this, you don't need:

• A huge audience
• An "original" idea
• Or years of experience

The secret?

Get uncomfortably specific—and then niche down even more.

Here's how (in 4 dead-simple steps):

1. Pick an industry → a niche → a niche-within-a-niche.

Most people start too broad.

Mega-categories like "Healthcare" or "Technology" are enormous.

You can't pinpoint a real problem inside one of them.

You have to niche down 3 levels deep by:

• Industry
• Demographic
• Platform
• Location
• Or price point

For example:

"Healthcare" → "Physical Therapy" → "Physical Therapists On LinkedIn."

The more specific you go, the higher-quality your ideas become.

(Don't skip Step 1—your ideas will be vague if you do.)

2. List the 10 Biggest Problems in your niche-within-niche.

Your niche-within-niche should be something you've personally experienced.

When Cole and I started Ship 30, our niche was:

"Beginner writers starting online."

Their 10 biggest problems:

• Distractions
• Over-Editing
• Perfectionism
• Procrastination
• Self-Confidence
• Generating Ideas
• Imposter Syndrome
• Writing Consistently
• Choosing A Platform
• Finding Time To Write

3. List the 10 Most Desirable Outcomes.

Take your problem list and flip each one.

Write them as "I" statements.

This puts yourself in the customer's shoes.

Here's what we came up with for Ship 30:

• "No more distractions, so I can finally be productive"

• "No more over-editing, so I can publish way more often"

• "No more perfectionism holding me back from writing full-time"

• "No more procrastination, so I can make real progress toward my writing goals"

• "More self-confidence, so I can finally share the insights I've been afraid to publish"

• "A reliable system for generating ideas, so I'm never stuck staring at a blank page"

• "No more imposter syndrome, so I can stop comparing myself to others and feel fulfilled"

• "Writing consistently, so my audience and library grow faster"

• "Knowing which platform to write on, so I can finally start my journey online"

• "More time to write, so I can grow my audience and feel like I'm on the right path"


This exercise is revealing.

And you'll use these in your marketing later.

4. Pick the most painful problem and build your product around it.

Don't lead with all 10 problems.

Lead with one.

If everything is a priority, nothing is.

One problem.
One product.
One clear solution.

The little-known technique top writers use to create and sell products without tons of additional work:

Wealthy writers like:

• Malcolm Gladwell
• Ryan Holiday
• Mark Manson

Use this underrated framework to build massive audiences—and make millions every year.

So, what is this little-known but highly lucrative skill?

Lean Writing.

It's where you take a piece of proven content and expand it into a longer-form asset:

→ Atomic Essay
→ Newsletter
→ Books
→ Digital Product

But how do you expand an idea into all of the above assets?

Use the 10 Magical Ways.

Every idea can be expanded by adding:

1. Tips
2. Lessons
3. Benefits
4. Reasons
5. Mistakes
6. Examples
7. Questions
8. Data Points
9. Personal Stories
10. Step-By-Step Frameworks

But how do you get started?

Follow these 4 simple steps:

1. Start with what's already working on social media.

There’s a treasure trove of validated ideas EVERYWHERE, just waiting to be expanded:

• An IG story that got thousands of likes
• Or a Twitter thread that went semi-viral
• Or a LinkedIn post that got 500 comments

So look at your own analytics.

There will be data points telling you *exactly* what your readers want more of.

2. Take these ideas and expand them into Atomic Essays.

Now you're testing if people want to hear the expanded version.

Here’s how to write an Atomic Essay:

• Craft an intriguing headline.
• Outline the key points of the core message of your essay.
• Expand on your main points.

If you can write an Atomic Essay, you can write anything.

3. Create a weekly newsletter based on successful posts.

Again, you're just expanding your proven ideas.

But how do you know what to write in the newsletter? 

Easy!

• Have a look in your analytics
• Take a proven idea and expand it into a weekly newsletter
• Expand the idea with the 10 Magical Ways (as I described above)

And to make it 10x quicker, use AI to help you.

4. Scale into paid digital products and courses.

You already have all the foundational content:

• The social posts proved the initial interest
• The Atomic Essays provided the detailed framework
• The newsletters validated the idea further

And now, you're just expanding into a premium offering—a product!
